This module discusses imperfectly competitive labor markets.

1. Firm wage premia#

If firms have no wage-setting power, firm wage premia would not exist for equally productive workers. Here we discuss that firm wage premia exist and are important for explaining wage inequality.

Card, Heining and Kline (2013)#

The paper discussed is Card, Heining and Kline (2013), “Workplace Heterogeneity and the Rise of West German Wage Inequality”, Quarterly Journal of Economics. The discussion explains the AKM estimator to identify worker and firm types in matched employer-employee data and shows the importance of firm wage premia and sorting in explaining wage inequality.
